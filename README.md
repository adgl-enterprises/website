## The short version

We’re a **Colorado Springs** technology company at the intersection of **blockchain R&D**, **ASIC and silicon-oriented IP**, and the **crypto ecosystem**. We’re operators and builders: small team energy, serious engineering, and a bias toward shipping ideas that can survive contact with real networks and real hardware constraints.

**ADGL** stands for **A Damn Good Life** — not a throwaway slogan, but a constraint on how we work. We build hard tech *because* we believe ambitious problems deserve ambitious teams, and that the work itself should be worth showing up for.

---

## Why we exist

Decentralized systems stop being theoretical the moment they hit **consensus**, **latency**, and **economics** at scale. The next chapter of that story is as much about **specialized hardware** and **protocol-level design** as it is about app layers. ADGL exists to push on those layers early — research and IP **before** the market fully agrees on the shape of the stack.

We’re not trying to be everything to everyone. We’re trying to be **sharp** in a few places where depth beats breadth.

## How we work (the startup compact)

- **Default to clarity.** Direct communication beats hierarchy. If it’s ambiguous, we make it legible.
- **Lean execution.** Small batches, tight feedback loops, and a distaste for theater. Progress should be visible.
- **Long-term bets, short-term honesty.** We’re not afraid of multi-year R&D; we *are* afraid of pretending a prototype is production.
- **Colorado as a base, not a postcard.** Colorado Springs is home — high-trust relationships, room to think, and a culture that rewards builders who finish things.
